ABBstract

Cooperative Robotics

This project frames human-robot interaction as a co-creative performance. The robots collaborate with two humans in a timed painting exchange, offering wooden blocks to be painted before retrieving and stacking them into a choreographed composition.

While the process appears improvisational, the structure is encoded, placing robotic motion at the center of aesthetic decision-making. The final artwork is not predefined but emerges through interaction: a tangible artifact of shared authorship between human intuition and machine logic.

The path is planned differently for both robots. The PET Bot approaches from a lower angle, following soft, curved motion paths that feel intuitive and accommodating, allowing users to interact with ease. In contrast, the POWER Bot moves assertively from the front, tracing dominant, arched trajectories that often challenge the user's space and alter the dynamics of co-creation.

Horizon

Telepresence Device

Horizon is a responsive picture frame that displays the sky gradient of any location the user wishes to experience. It offers a moment of telepresence, allowing users to engage in the simple act of watching the sky from anywhere in the world.

The family of devices comprises two main elements: the frame and an audio device. These incorporate a NeoPixel assembly, an acrylic overlay, and a speaker to recreate sky gradients and ambient soundscapes.

Windows give a sneak peek into the transient world. They frame views, skylines, and beautiful sky gradients. This project delves into creating the feeling of being in a different place (telepresence) and experiencing the ever-changing skylines of various locations.

Influx

Transformable Façade

Influx is a dynamic surface that choreographs the balance between light and shade. Through transformable structures and iridescent materials, it playfully filters light, sometimes letting it in, sometimes holding it back—creating an ever-changing interplay of color and shadow that produces vibrant, shaded space.

Shaded Interiors

Daylight Modulation

Radiative Cooling

This system uses simple movements and materials to modulate light softly and responsively. The project evolved as a result of the question, "How might we design a dynamic system that integrates auxetic transformation as a geometrical response to light which results in shading, combined with materials that produce visual color variation effects?"

Initial tests were carried out with foam as material. To scale the project, conventional movement technique such as hinge is done in combination with auxetic pattern. The movement takes place in 2 states, open and closed state of the mechanism.
